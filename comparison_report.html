Why Our System Targets Better Accuracy Unlike P-only controllers that always leave a small steady-state error, our PI (Proportional-Integral) controller continuously learns from past errors and eliminates offset. The EKF further predicts incoming heat loads from occupancy changes before they affect temperature — making the controller proactive, not just reactive.

Three Mechanisms Behind Energy Savings (1) Demand-Controlled Ventilation: Fresh air flow scales with actual CO₂ readings — not a fixed schedule. Studies show 15–20% ventilation energy savings with DCV.   (2) No Overcooling: Each zone's PI controller tracks its exact setpoint independently — Zone 4 (Server Room) runs at 21°C with a minimum airflow floor, preventing the wasteful overcooling common in single-setpoint systems.   (3) Night Setback: Occupancy-aware scheduling reduces airflow to near-zero during off-hours and weekends.
3. Indoor Air Quality — CO₂ Levels
Peak CO₂ concentration during peak occupancy hours. ASHRAE 62.1 recommends < 1000 ppm (or 700 ppm above outdoor)
Building / System Type Typical Peak CO₂ (ppm) ASHRAE 62.1 CO₂ Controlled?
Typical Sri Lankan Office (Fixed OA Schedule) 1200 – 1800 ppm ❌ Exceeds No
Smart Thermostats (Nest, Ecobee) 900 – 1400 ppm ⚠️ Sometimes No (temperature only)
Commercial BMS (CO₂ sensor add-on) 700 – 900 ppm ✅ Usually Yes (costly add-on)
Team Ventus — Proposed System Target: < 800 ppm ✅ Compliant Yes — built-in, per zone
CO₂ is a First-Class Control Variable in Our System The central AHU coordinator reads CO₂ from all five zones every timestep and dynamically adjusts outdoor air flow to the worst-case zone. This directly targets ASHRAE 62.1 compliance — without any costly additional sensor network or BMS licence. The EKF also estimates CO₂ generation rate, enabling proactive ventilation before levels peak.
